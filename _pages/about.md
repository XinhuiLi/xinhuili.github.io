My name is Xinhui Li (黎欣惠). I am a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at [Georgia Institute of Technology](https://coe.gatech.edu/) and [Center for Translational Research in Neuroimaging and Data Science (TReNDS)](https://trendscenter.org/), advised by [Prof. Vince D. Calhoun](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=WNOoGKIAAAAJ&hl=en) and [Dr. Rogers F. Silva](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=cMtwwG8AAAAJ&hl=en).

I work at the intersection of machine learning and computational neuroscience, developing methods and tools for large-scale neuroimaging analysis to better understand the brain and its disorders.
- Multimodal Representation Learning: I develop multimodal fusion methods that learn latent representations from high-dimensional, multimodal neuroimaging data, with a focus on identifying phenotypic and neuropsychiatric biomarkers.
  - Related work: [subspace independent vector analysis](https://doi.org/10.1162/IMAG.a.1266), [multimodal independent vector analysis](https://doi.org/10.1002/hbm.70037), [Torch-MISA](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10230492)
- AI for Mental Health: I design generative models, agentic systems, and neuroimaging-based metrics for characterizing and assessing neuropsychiatric disorders, with the goal of improving diagnosis and treatment of mental illness.
  - Related work: [functional network connectivity interpolation](https://doi.org/10.1162/IMAG.a.1220), [AI psychiatrist assistant](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v297/greene26a.html), [brainwide risk score](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0006322323015925)
- Open and Reproducible Science: As a first-generation scholar, I am committed to creating an open, collaborative research culture. I systematically study variability in neuroimaging preprocessing pipelines and contribute to open-source tools and standards to improve the reproducibility and reliability of neuroimaging analysis.
  - Related work: [interpipeline agreement](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-024-01942-4), [C-PAC](https://fcp-indi.github.io/), [NMIND](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-023-01647-0)
- Cross-Species Neuroimaging: I build deep learning models for brain extraction and segmentation across species, including non-human primate MRI.
  - Related work: [DeepBet](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2021.118001), [DeepSeg](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10781829)
